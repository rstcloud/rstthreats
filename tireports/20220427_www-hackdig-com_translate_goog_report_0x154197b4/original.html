    Suspected Manlinghua APT group disguised as multinational identities to attack Bangladesh

    2022-04-26 18:42

     

    Bitter is an APT organization that is widely believed to be from India. The organization has long launched cyber attacks against government, military, power, nuclear and other departments in my country and surrounding South Asian countries, stealing sensitive data, and has a strong political background. .

    Recently, the Forensic Lab of Anheng Security Data Department captured a number of activity samples suspected of Manlinghua organization. This batch of samples is very similar to the previous attack activities of the organization in terms of attack methods or weapon codes, and continues its consistent attack characteristics.

    The Manlinghua organization used the authorized Pakistan and Bangladesh government mailboxes to launch network attacks. We found that one of the backlink domain names used Chinese pinyin, which was suspected to be disguised as China to attack.

    Disguised domain emails exposed

    This time, Forensic Hunting Lab captured multiple email samples. The recipients of these emails are the mailboxes of Bangladeshi government agencies. According to the senders, they can be divided into two categories: one is sent from the mailbox accounts of Pakistani government agencies, and the other is sent from the mailbox accounts of Pakistani government agencies. Type I emails are sent from Bangladesh government agency email accounts.

    01 Mail from Pakistan:

    In this batch of samples, the earliest time of sending is September 1, 2021, from dddata_int@rab.gov.bd, the sender's name and email address are disguised as rab 3 tikatuly (RAB-3 tikatuly troops )

    This batch of emails used different official email accounts from Pakistan, disguised as the email accounts of the Rapid Response Force (RAB), and carried out phishing activities. What these emails have in common is the use of the same camouflage designation, all disguised as normal applications from RAB troops in other camps in Bangladesh.
